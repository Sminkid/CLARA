Project Brief — SmartPatch v1.0 SmartPatch is a wearable biosensor patch designed to continuously monitor a patient's heart rate, skin temperature, and SpO2 (blood oxygen saturation) in a post-operative hospital ward setting. The patch adheres to the patient's chest and transmits data wirelessly to a bedside tablet application used by nursing staff. Key requirements: The patch must sample heart rate, temperature, and SpO2 every 30 seconds and transmit readings to the tablet via Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE). The tablet application must display live readings, store a 72-hour rolling history, and alert nursing staff if any reading falls outside clinically defined thresholds. The patch must be biocompatible, single-use, and rated for a minimum of 72 hours of continuous wear. The system must comply with relevant Australian medical device regulations (TGA) and the IEC 62304 standard for medical device software. Alert thresholds must be configurable by a clinical administrator but must not be adjustable by nursing staff during active monitoring. The patch must operate without recharging for the full 72-hour wear period. Patient data must be stored securely and must not leave the hospital's local network.
